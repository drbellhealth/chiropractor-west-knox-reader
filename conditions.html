Conditions Commonly Treated by a Knoxville Chiropractor

Most of what a west-side chiropractor treats is muscle-and-joint stuff. It responds to conservative care. Here's the common list. Right after it: the honest part about when a chiropractor isn't your first call.

The Most Common Reasons People Come In

  • Lower back pain. New or long-running.
  • Neck pain and stiffness. Including 'tech neck.'
  • Sciatica. Disc pain that shoots down your leg.
  • Tension and cervicogenic headaches.
  • Migraines. As extra, supporting care.
  • Mid-back pain and rib restrictions.
  • Hip, knee, and ankle stiffness.
  • Shoulder, elbow, and wrist pain.
  • Whiplash from a car wreck on the interstate.
  • Sports injuries and overuse problems.
  • Posture aches from sitting at a desk.
  • Back and pelvic pain during pregnancy.

Lower Back Pain

Lower back pain is the number-one reason west-siders see a chiropractor. Most of the time it's mechanical. A pulled muscle. A stiff joint. A cranky facet. A disc bulging a little. That kind responds to adjustments, soft-tissue work, sometimes decompression, and a few home exercises. And the same exam catches the rare serious cause and sends you on.

Neck Pain & Tech Neck

Neck pain is the second most common complaint. These days it's mostly screens. The fix is usually gentle adjustments. Plus loosening the tight muscles up top. Plus posture tips you take home. Most people feel better in just a few visits.

Sciatica & Disc-Related Pain

'Sciatica' means leg pain that starts at a nerve in your lower back. Usually a bulging or herniated disc. Sometimes a tight muscle or a cranky joint. Conservative care clears most cases in a few weeks. But watch for warning signs. Growing weakness. Numbness. Losing control of your bladder or bowels. That's urgent. Get checked right away.

Headaches & Migraines

Tension and cervicogenic headaches start in a tight neck. They respond well to chiropractic. Migraines are different. They vary. A good office tells you straight whether it's worth continuing. And works with your doctor if needed.

Auto Accident, Sports & Work Injuries

Car wrecks and work injuries bring a lot of people in. No surprise with the traffic on the I-40/I-75 split. Good news on cost: auto-injury care is often paid by the other driver's insurance or your own med-pay. The office documents it. Treats it. And deals with your insurer or attorney. Sports injuries follow the same path.

When to See a Chiropractor vs. an MD

In Tennessee you don't need a referral. Chiropractors are direct-access. But some things belong elsewhere first. Red-flag back pain with fever. Weight loss you can't explain. Bad night pain. Anything neurological. A possible fracture or stroke. Any real emergency. A good chiropractor spots those fast. And sends you on instead of treating the wrong thing.
